How I Started My Programming Journey?

April 13, 2025

At 13, I was introduced to Java as part of my school curriculum. Honestly, my first reaction was, "What the hell is this, & why are we even studying it?" I had zero interest in learning it back then. It felt like just another subject to pass. I didn’t understand what it was for or how it could ever be useful to me.

In 2020, during the COVID lockdown, a friend shared a YouTube video with me. It was a guy designing some slick neumorphism-style website with neon animation. I was blown away. I had no clue what he was doing or how any of it worked, but I knew one thing. I wanted to do it too.

I followed the tutorial, installed an IDE (VS Code), and started copying the code line by line. It was a struggle writing code, pausing, debugging, rewinding. Frustration was real. But when I finally got it working, I felt like a God.

The Moment Of No Return.

From that day on, I started hunting for more videos like that. I’d copy paste code & play around with it mostly just tweaking the innerHTML because that’s all I understood. But after repeating the same cycle of watch, copy, customize for a while, I hit a point where I wanted more control more customization. And YouTube didn’t have answers for that.

So I decided to learn CSS myself. I began watching tutorials everything from designing individual components to full-blown websites. At first, my mindset was still to just copy & paste. But slowly, things started clicking. I began to understand what each CSS property was doing, not just blindly using it. Back then, my approach was to pick a project idea & start building & learning everything I needed as I went.

I built a lot of websites during that time, pouring all my creativity into them. But eventually I realized I was missing something JavaScript. At first, I assumed JavaScript was just like Java (I mean, the names are so similar), & since my relationship with Java wasn’t exactly a love story, I avoided JS for a while.

But eventually, I told myself, "I can’t ignore this anymore". So I dove into the basics of JavaScript not too deep at first, just enough to write simple scripts & understand what I was doing. I started integrating those small pieces into my websites. The results were exciting. It felt like my static pages had come to life. That push gave me enough momentum to keep learning JavaScript as I built more projects learning just what I needed, exactly when I needed it.

Keep Moving Forward.

As time went on, I eventually made the decision to revisit Java. Surprisingly, this time it felt much easier & honestly, I felt like an idiot for struggling with it so much back then. I could now write basic programs & even understand complex code without much trouble. But one thing became clear my logic building skills needed work.

So instead of chasing quantity, I focused on quality. I’d learn less but understand deeply, experimenting with code until it made complete sense. I wouldn’t say Java became a piece of cake, but I can say with confidence I wasn’t the same guy I used to be.

After Java, my curiosity pushed me to explore more. I learned C++, Python, C, React.js, Next.js, etc. Each new technology I tried gave me a different perspective & a fresh challenge.

Now, I’m at a stage where I want to dive deeper into fields like Cloud Computing, Data Science, DevOps, AI & ML, & more. I don’t have it all figured out yet, but I’m enjoying the journey. With time, experience, & exploration, I’ll eventually find what truly excites me.

Right now, my main focus is on mastering Data Structures & Algorithms. It's one of the best ways to improve logic-building & write cleaner, more efficient code. Learning DSA has taught me how to think critically about time & space complexity, which is a must have skill when building scalable applications.

You Are Who You Choose To Be.

I wasn't born a programmer I chose to become one. Every late night spent debugging, every tutorial watched, every frustrating error fixed was a decision to grow. I’ve realized that passion alone isn’t enough; it’s the choices we make every day that shape who we become. I chose to be curious, to keep learning, and to never settle and that made all the difference.
